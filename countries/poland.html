Day-by-day

Your route in Poland

DAY 1

Newcastle to Krakow

Fly via Amsterdam, take the airport train to Krakow Glowny, check in, then walk Rynek Glowny, the Cloth Hall and St Mary's Basilica.

Krakow and Zakopane
DAY 2

Krakow to Zakopane

Visit Wawel Hill and Kazimierz, then take the evening coach from Krakow MDA to Zakopane. Check in and walk Krupowki Street.

Krakow and Zakopane
DAY 3

Zakopane

Choose Gubalowka and the town, or the thermal baths. Treat Morskie Oko as a serious winter plan, not a casual walk.

Krakow and Zakopane
DAY 4

Zakopane to Prague

Coach back to Krakow, keep at least a two-hour buffer, then take the train via Ostrava to Prague.

Transport

Flights, trains and buses

  • Newcastle-Amsterdam-Krakow flight: £98.97 historical reference; keep it on one ticket.
  • Krakow Airport to Krakow Glowny: airport train, allow £4-£6.
  • Krakow MDA to Zakopane: coach 17:00-19:00, £18.45 historical reference.
  • Zakopane to Krakow: coach 08:40-10:40, allow £8-£15 and protect the Prague train with a long buffer.
Food

What to eat

  • Krakow: obwarzanek, pierogi, zapiekanka and zurek.
  • Zakopane: oscypek cheese, potato pancakes and mountain-style soup.
  • Daily allowance: £20-£32 per day.
Activities

Best use of time

  • Rynek Glowny and Cloth Hall: Historic-centre walk; the square is free and museum sections cost extra.
  • Wawel Hill and Kazimierz: Use the free grounds and streets; choose paid interiors selectively.
  • Gubalowka: Mountain viewpoint; allow roughly £8-£12 depending on the live fare.
  • Chocholowskie thermal baths: Bad-weather option; allow about £25-£40 including transfer.
  • Morskie Oko: Attempt only with safe weather, winter footwear, enough daylight and a proper plan.
