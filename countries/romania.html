Base: Bucharest

Flight plan: London to Bucharest Otopeni (OTP), direct return flight

Hotel plan: University Square, Old Town edge or Piața Romană; 2 nights

Three-day itinerary

Day What I will do Transport and buses
Day 1
Arrival and old centre		 I will use the airport train or express bus, check in, then walk the Old Town, Stavropoleos Church, CEC Palace exterior and Revolution Square. Airport train or express bus; Metro and walking.
Day 2
Palace and thermal choice		 I will pre-book the Palace of Parliament, then visit the Village Museum and Herăstrău Park. In the evening I will choose Therme București if I want a longer paid activity. Metro, tram or bus; Therme transfer bus/taxi if selected.
Day 3
Market and departure		 I will visit Obor Market or the Romanian Athenaeum area, have lunch and return to OTP. Metro plus airport train/bus.

Transport, local travel and buses

  • Airport: the rail link to Gara de Nord is reliable; an express bus is

    useful if it stops closer to my hotel.

  • Local: Metro is the fastest option; STB buses and trams will cover

    parks and neighbourhoods. I will validate the correct ticket or travel card.

  • Long excursion: Brașov and Bran can be done, but combining both with

    flight days would turn this into a rushed box-ticking trip, so they are not in the core plan.

Hotel, food and extra activities

Food plan: I will reserve £18–£30 per day. Two examples are a bakery or soup lunch and a casual Romanian dinner with polenta, chicken, vegetables or stuffed cabbage.

Extra activity 1: Therme București: allow £25–£45 including transfer and chosen zone.

Extra activity 2: Guided Palace of Parliament visit: allow £15–£25 and carry the required original identification.
