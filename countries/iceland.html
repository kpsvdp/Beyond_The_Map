Reykjavík: flights, room, food, transport and activities

Base: Reykjavík

Flight plan: London to Keflavík (KEF), direct return flight

Hotel plan: Hlemmur or central Reykjavík; 2 nights in a guesthouse or basic hotel

Three-day itinerary

Day What I will do Transport and buses
Day 1
Arrival and Reykjavík		 I will take the airport coach to Reykjavík, walk Hallgrímskirkja, Laugavegur, Harpa and the harbour, then eat an early dinner. Flybus or Airport Direct; walking; Strætó city bus if the weather is poor.
Day 2
Golden Circle		 I will use a full-day coach for Þingvellir, Geysir and Gullfoss. This will be the most efficient use of one full day without hiring a car. Pre-booked sightseeing coach; hotel or central bus-stop pickup.
Day 3
Lagoon or city museum		 I will choose Sky Lagoon or the National Museum, have lunch in the centre and take the airport coach back to KEF. City bus/taxi to the lagoon if selected; airport coach booked around the flight.

Transport, local travel and buses

  • Airport: KEF is about 50 km from Reykjavík; there is no city train, so

    I will pre-book a coach and avoid an expensive airport taxi.

  • Local: Reykjavík is walkable. Strætó buses will cover bad-weather

    journeys; I will use the app or a contactless option accepted at the time of travel.

  • Tour bus: a Golden Circle coach is more realistic than public buses

    for a three-day plan because the rural stops are spread out.

Hotel, food and extra activities

Food plan: I will reserve £35–£55 per day and use a supermarket for breakfast. Two manageable meals are a soup-and-bread lunch and a food-hall dinner; repeated restaurant meals will push the trip far above budget.

Extra activity 1: Sky Lagoon: allow roughly £55–£90 depending on package and transfer.

Extra activity 2: Northern Lights coach in season: allow £45–£75, with no guarantee of a sighting.
