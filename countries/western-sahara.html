Dakhla: flights, room, food, transport and activities

A separate London-based plan for one adult, written as a future trip. Recheck live prices and schedules before paying.

Hard truth

This is technically possible but a poor three-day choice from London. Connections and disruption risk can consume most of the trip.

Base: Dakhla, Moroccan-administered area

Flight plan: London to Casablanca (CMN), then Casablanca to Dakhla (VIL)

Hotel plan: Dakhla town for value or a lagoon camp for an activity-led stay; 2 nights

Three-day itinerary

Day What I will do Transport and buses
Day 1
Long journey and arrival		 I will connect through Casablanca, use a pre-arranged airport transfer in Dakhla, check in and keep the evening near the hotel. Two flights; hotel transfer/taxi. There is no useful rail option.
Day 2
Lagoon excursion		 I will join a licensed operator for the lagoon, White Dune or Dragon Island area, with a clear return time and weather plan. Operator 4x4/minibus and boat only where conditions permit.
Day 3
Town and departure		 I will visit Dakhla's waterfront and local market, then return to VIL for the Casablanca connection. Taxi/hotel shuttle and two-flight return.

Transport, local travel and buses

  • Reality check: like Greenland, this is poor value for a strict

    three-day trip. Connections consume much of Day 1 and Day 3.

  • Local: public transport is limited for tourist sites; I will

    pre-arrange transfers and use an organised excursion rather than improvise desert travel.

  • Safety: I will not travel near the Berm or Es Semara. FCDO currently

    advises against all travel within 40 km north and west of the Berm, including Es Semara, and all territory south/east of it.

Hotel, food and extra activities

Food plan: I will reserve £20–£35 per day and confirm whether the hotel or camp includes meals. Two simple choices are a café lunch in town and a pre-arranged hotel/camp dinner.

Extra activity 1: Lagoon/White Dune 4x4 excursion: allow £70–£160 depending on private or shared format.

Extra activity 2: Kitesurf lesson: allow £60–£120; only with a reputable school, proper equipment and suitable conditions.
