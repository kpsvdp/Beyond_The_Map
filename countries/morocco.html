Base: Marrakech

Flight plan: London to Marrakech (RAK), direct return flight

Hotel plan: Medina edge for atmosphere or Gueliz for simpler transport; 2 nights

Three-day itinerary

Day What I will do Transport and buses
Day 1
Arrival and Jemaa el-Fna		 I will take the airport bus or a pre-agreed taxi, check in, then explore Koutoubia, the souks and Jemaa el-Fna before choosing a rooftop dinner. Airport bus; walking; petit taxi for longer city hops.
Day 2
Palaces and garden		 I will visit Bahia Palace and Ben Youssef Madrasa early, then choose Jardin Majorelle/YSL Museum or a hammam in the afternoon. Walking in the Medina; city bus or metered taxi to Majorelle.
Day 3
Short excursion and departure		 I will use the morning for Gueliz, Menara Gardens or a half-day Agafay activity only if the flight is late enough, then return to RAK. City bus/taxi; organised minibus for Agafay.

Transport, local travel and buses

  • Airport: the official bus is economical; if I use a taxi I will agree

    the fare at the official rank or use a hotel transfer.

  • Local: much of the Medina is pedestrian. City buses and petit taxis

    will handle Gueliz, Majorelle and the airport corridor.

  • Excursion bus: an organised small-group minibus is the realistic way

    to reach Agafay or the Atlas foothills within a short visit.

Hotel, food and extra activities

Food plan: I will reserve £18–£32 per day. Two affordable examples are msemen or a café breakfast and a chicken/vegetable tagine or couscous dinner away from the main square's highest-priced terraces.

Extra activity 1: Hammam: allow £20–£60 depending on local or spa-style treatment.

Extra activity 2: Agafay half-day/sunset excursion: allow £30–£70; avoid operators with unclear animal-welfare or safety standards.
