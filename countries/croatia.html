Base: Dubrovnik

Flight plan: London to Dubrovnik (DBV), usually direct in the main travel season

Hotel plan: Gruž or Lapad for better value; 2 nights

Three-day itinerary

Day What I will do Transport and buses
Day 1
Arrival and Old Town		 I will use the airport shuttle bus, leave my bag, then walk Stradun, the harbour and the quieter lanes before sunset. Airport shuttle bus to Pile/Gruž; local Libertas bus.
Day 2
Walls and island choice		 I will walk the city walls early, then choose a Lokrum ferry or a guided sea-kayak session. I will return before evening winds increase. Local bus; ferry or kayak operator.
Day 3
Cable car viewpoint and departure		 I will take the cable car or hike to the lower viewpoint, have lunch, collect my bag and use the airport bus. Libertas bus and airport shuttle.

Transport, local travel and buses

  • Airport: shuttle buses are the budget choice; I will check the

    timetable against the flight because departures follow seasonal schedules.

  • Local: Libertas buses link Gruž, Lapad and Pile. The historic centre

    itself is pedestrian-only.

  • Season warning: direct London flights and many sea activities are

    seasonal; outside that period I may need a connection or a Zagreb-based plan.

Hotel, food and extra activities

Food plan: I will reserve £27–£43 per day and avoid eating every meal on Stradun. Two better-value choices are a Gruž bakery/market lunch and a casual pizza, pasta or grilled-chicken dinner in Lapad.

Extra activity 1: Lokrum ferry and reserve entry: allow £25–£35 in season.

Extra activity 2: Sea kayaking: allow £30–£55 depending on length and sunset timing.
