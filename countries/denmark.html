Copenhagen: flights, room, food, transport and activities

A separate London-based plan for one adult, written as a future trip. Recheck live prices and schedules before paying.

🛂 Visa routeSchengen visa

Base: Copenhagen

Flight plan: London to Copenhagen (CPH), direct return flight

Hotel plan: Vesterbro, Nørrebro or near the central station; 2 nights

Three-day itinerary

Day What I will do Transport and buses
Day 1
Arrival and harbour		 I will take the Metro or train from CPH, check in, then walk Nyhavn, Kongens Nytorv, Strøget and the waterfront. Airport Metro/train; walking; harbour bus if useful.
Day 2
Royal Copenhagen		 I will visit Rosenborg Castle and the King's Garden, continue to Amalienborg and take a canal tour. In the evening I will choose Tivoli if it is open. Metro, S-train and city bus; canal boat for the activity.
Day 3
Christianshavn and departure		 I will walk Christianshavn, climb the Church of Our Saviour if open, eat lunch at a food market and return to the airport. Metro direct to CPH; local bus only if luggage makes walking awkward.

Transport, local travel and buses

  • Airport: Metro and regional trains will both reach central Copenhagen

    quickly; I will use the option closest to my hotel.

  • Local: DOT tickets cover Metro, buses, harbour buses and trains within

    the selected zones. A 24- or 48-hour City Pass may beat single tickets.

  • Buses: regular city buses will fill short gaps, while the harbour bus

    will double as low-cost sightseeing.

Hotel, food and extra activities

Food plan: I will reserve £30–£48 per day. Two cost-controlled choices are bakery breakfast plus smørrebrød at lunch, and a food-hall or casual Middle Eastern dinner.

Extra activity 1: Tivoli Gardens: allow £20–£55 depending on entry and ride package.

Extra activity 2: Canal tour: allow £15–£25 for a standard harbour circuit.
