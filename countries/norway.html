Base: Oslo

Flight plan: London to Oslo Gardermoen (OSL), direct return flight

Hotel plan: Sentrum, Grønland or Grünerløkka; 2 nights

Three-day itinerary

Day What I will do Transport and buses
Day 1
Arrival and waterfront		 I will take a train from OSL, then walk the Opera House, Bjørvika waterfront, Akershus Fortress and Aker Brygge. Airport train or Vy regional train; walking; tram if tired.
Day 2
Museums and fjord		 I will visit the National Museum or MUNCH, then take an Oslofjord sightseeing boat or use the public ferry to the islands. Ruter tram, Metro, bus and ferry; pre-booked cruise only if selected.
Day 3
Vigeland and departure		 I will visit Vigeland Park, stop for lunch in Grünerløkka, collect my bag and return to OSL. Tram/Metro; airport train. I will avoid flights marketed as Oslo if they use distant Torp unless the saving is large.

Transport, local travel and buses

  • Airport: Vy regional trains are often cheaper than Flytoget; both use

    the same main rail corridor from OSL.

  • Local: Ruter tickets cover city buses, trams, Metro and local ferries

    in the chosen zones.

  • Budget warning: Torp (TRF) is far from Oslo and the transfer can erase

    a cheap airfare saving.

Hotel, food and extra activities

Food plan: I will reserve £32–£52 per day and use a supermarket or bakery for breakfast. Two sensible options are a food-hall lunch and an early casual dinner rather than a full-service restaurant twice daily.

Extra activity 1: Oslofjord sightseeing cruise: allow £30–£50.

Extra activity 2: Sauna by the fjord: allow £20–£35 and book a timed session.
