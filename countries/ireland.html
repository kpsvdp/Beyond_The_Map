Base: Dublin

Flight plan: London to Dublin (DUB), direct return flight; ferry is an alternative

Hotel plan: Smithfield, Drumcondra or Docklands; 2 nights

Three-day itinerary

Day What I will do Transport and buses
Day 1
Arrival and city centre		 I will take an airport bus, leave my bag, then walk the River Liffey, Temple Bar lanes, Dublin Castle exterior and St Stephen's Green. Airport coach or Dublin Bus; walking; Luas if needed.
Day 2
Books, history and coast		 I will pre-book the Book of Kells/Trinity experience or Kilmainham Gaol, then take DART to Howth for a harbour walk if the weather is reasonable. Dublin Bus/Luas; DART to Howth.
Day 3
Museum and departure		 I will visit a free national museum or the Guinness Storehouse, have lunch and take the airport bus back to DUB. Bus/Luas and airport coach.

Transport, local travel and buses

  • Airport: Dublin has no airport rail line; I will compare an express

    coach with public bus routes based on my hotel.

  • Local: Leap/contactless options cover Dublin Bus, Luas and DART under

    current rules; I will use public transport instead of central taxis.

  • Alternative: a rail-and-ferry route can avoid flying but consumes much

    more of a three-day trip.

Hotel, food and extra activities

Food plan: I will reserve £27–£43 per day. Two practical options are a deli/soup lunch and an early pub or casual restaurant dinner outside the Temple Bar price zone.

Extra activity 1: Guinness Storehouse: allow £25–£35 depending on slot.

Extra activity 2: Book of Kells Experience: allow £20–£30 and book ahead.
