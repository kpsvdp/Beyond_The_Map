🇳🇱 Netherlands · Days 4–6

Two Amsterdam nights and the Schengen exit

Travel direct from Paris to Amsterdam, spend a full day around the canals and museums, then leave the Schengen area from Schiphol on the flight to Newcastle.

Amsterdam itinerary

Days 4–6

DAY 4

Paris → Amsterdam

Direct Eurostar in about 3h 20m, hotel check-in and an evening canal walk. Arrive at the Paris terminal at least 20 minutes early.

£35–£110
DAY 5

Canals, Jordaan and Museumplein

Take a canal cruise, walk Jordaan and Nine Streets, then choose Rijksmuseum or free Vondelpark. Finish around Dam Square.

Full day
DAY 6

Central walk and flight home

Begijnhof, flower market or another free central walk, then collect bags, take the train to Schiphol and allow about three hours before the Newcastle flight.

Exit Schengen
Room

Amsterdam accommodation

  • Stay: 2 continuous nights, plan £160–£280.
  • Areas: Sloterdijk or Oost may offer better value than the canal core.
  • City tax: often charged separately; keep a trip-wide £20–£50 allowance for hotel taxes.
  • Visa proof: the hotel must cover both nights and display the applicant’s full name and dates.
Transport

Train, GVB and Schiphol

  • Paris → Amsterdam: plan £35–£110.
  • City travel: the source guide listed a 2-day Amsterdam Travel Ticket at €27.
  • Airport: Amsterdam Centraal–Schiphol train about 17 minutes, from €5.50.
  • Flight: Amsterdam–Newcastle estimate £60–£160.
Food

Amsterdam meal plan

  • Day 4: station meal and a vegetarian or chicken dinner after check-in.
  • Day 5: poffertjes, stroopwafel, vegetable toast or chicken satay; £40–£55.
  • Day 6: breakfast, sandwich and airport snack; £20–£30.
  • Note: ask specifically for vegetarian bitterballen if wanted.

Visa role

Do not apply through the Netherlands for this itinerary

The Netherlands has two nights, fewer than France’s three. Include the Amsterdam hotel and exit flight in the France application. Use the Dutch VFS process only if a future itinerary genuinely makes the Netherlands the main destination.
