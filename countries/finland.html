Helsinki: flights, room, food, transport and activities

A separate London-based plan for one adult, written as a future trip. Recheck live prices and schedules before paying.

💷 Base total£445–£820
🛂 Visa routeSchengen visa

Base: Helsinki

Flight plan: London to Helsinki (HEL), direct return flight

Hotel plan: Kamppi, Kallio or near the central station; 2 nights

Three-day itinerary

Day What I will do Transport and buses
Day 1
Arrival and centre		 I will take the airport train, then walk Senate Square, Helsinki Cathedral, Market Square and the Esplanadi. I/P airport train; tram for the return to the hotel.
Day 2
Suomenlinna and design		 I will take the public ferry to Suomenlinna, explore the sea fortress, then return for the Design District, Oodi Library and a sauna. HSL ferry, tram and bus; walking on the island.
Day 3
Temppeliaukio and departure		 I will visit the Rock Church or Amos Rex, eat lunch near Kamppi and take the train back to HEL. Tram/Metro and airport train.

Transport, local travel and buses

  • Airport: HSL trains connect HEL with the central station; I will buy

    the correct ABC-zone ticket.

  • Local: the same HSL system covers trams, buses, Metro, local trains

    and the Suomenlinna ferry.

  • Buses: I will use city buses for gaps outside the tram grid, not

    taxis, unless travelling very early.

Hotel, food and extra activities

Food plan: I will reserve £27–£45 per day. Two good-value ideas are a market-hall soup or salmon lunch and a casual buffet or food-court dinner.

Extra activity 1: Public sauna: allow £15–£30 depending on venue.

Extra activity 2: Amos Rex or another major museum: allow £15–£22.
