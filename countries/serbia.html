Base: Belgrade

Flight plan: London to Belgrade (BEG), direct return flight

Hotel plan: Stari Grad, Dorćol or near Republic Square; 2 nights

Three-day itinerary

Day What I will do Transport and buses
Day 1
Arrival and fortress		 I will take an airport bus, check in, walk Knez Mihailova and explore Belgrade Fortress and Kalemegdan at sunset. Airport bus 72 or A1/minibus if operating; walking.
Day 2
Museums and rivers		 I will visit the Nikola Tesla Museum or Museum of Yugoslavia, then walk the Sava waterfront and take an evening river cruise if the weather works. City bus, tram and trolleybus; pre-booked cruise.
Day 3
Zemun and departure		 I will use a bus to Zemun, climb to Gardoš for the view, have lunch and return for the airport journey. City bus plus airport bus.

Transport, local travel and buses

  • Airport: bus 72 is the lowest-cost route; A1/minibus or a fixed-price

    taxi is faster if my flight time is awkward.

  • Local: buses, trams and trolleybuses cover the city. I will verify the

    current ticketing method because Belgrade has changed fare systems in recent years.

  • Hotel registration: my hotel will normally register my stay; I will

    confirm this at check-in and keep the proof if issued.

Hotel, food and extra activities

Food plan: I will reserve £18–£30 per day. Two affordable choices are a bakery/burek lunch and a casual grill, bean stew or vegetable dish for dinner.

Extra activity 1: Danube/Sava cruise: allow £15–£30.

Extra activity 2: Nikola Tesla Museum guided entry: allow £7–£12 and check the language schedule.
