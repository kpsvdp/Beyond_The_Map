Base: Lisbon

Flight plan: London to Lisbon (LIS), direct return flight

Hotel plan: Baixa, Avenida, Anjos or Arroios; 2 nights in a simple private room

Three-day itinerary

Day What I will do Transport and buses
Day 1
Arrival and old Lisbon		 I will take the airport Metro to the centre, leave my bag at the hotel, then walk through Baixa, Rossio, Praça do Comércio and Alfama. I will finish at a miradouro for sunset. Airport Metro; walking; tram or bus only for the steep return.
Day 2
Belém and Sintra choice		 I will spend the morning in Belém for Jerónimos Monastery, the riverside and a pastel de nata. In the afternoon I will either continue slowly in Lisbon or use the suburban train for a focused Sintra visit. Tram/bus to Belém; train to Sintra if selected; local Sintra bus for the hill sites.
Day 3
Castle and departure		 I will visit São Jorge Castle or the National Tile Museum, eat an early lunch, collect my bag and return to the airport. Metro or airport bus; allow extra time for hills and weekend queues.

Transport, local travel and buses

  • Airport: the Red Line Metro will connect LIS with the city; one

    transfer will reach Baixa or Avenida.

  • Local: I will use contactless/occasional tickets on Metro, Carris

    trams and buses. Tram 28 is scenic but crowded, so I will board early or use bus 737 for the castle area.

  • Intercity option: the Lisbon–Sintra train is faster and cheaper than a

    tour; Sintra's local buses will handle the steep palace circuit.

Hotel, food and extra activities

Food plan: I will reserve £25–£40 per day for breakfast, one casual lunch, dinner and snacks. Two easy examples are a bifana or vegetarian soup for lunch and grilled chicken, bacalhau or a rice dish for dinner.

Extra activity 1: Fado performance in Alfama: allow £25–£45, more if dinner is included.

Extra activity 2: Sintra palace entry: allow £12–£20 per selected monument, plus train and local bus.
