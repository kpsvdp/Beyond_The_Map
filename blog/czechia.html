Day 4

The long journey to Prague

Coach to Krakow, rail change at Ostrava

I left Zakopane early by coach and returned to Krakow. I deliberately kept a long gap before the international train because traffic from the mountains can be unpredictable.

After reaching Krakow, I walked to the railway station, bought food for the journey and checked the departure board carefully. My route to Prague included a change at Ostrava.

It was a long journey, but train travel made it comfortable. I could sit back, watch the Polish countryside pass and notice the gradual change as the train entered Czechia.

I arrived at Prague hlavni nadrazi in the evening and used local transport to reach Globe Hostel Prague Adults Only. After checking in, I took only a short walk. Prague deserved an early start rather than a rushed first night.

Day 5

Prague before Vienna

Bridge, castle, Old Town and the onward train

I began at Charles Bridge while the city was still relatively quiet. Walking across early made a huge difference: I could see the statues, river and Prague Castle before the heaviest crowds arrived.

From Mala Strana, I climbed towards Prague Castle. The streets became steeper, but every turn gave me another view across the red rooftops. I walked through the castle area and returned towards the Old Town.

By lunchtime, I had reached Old Town Square and the Astronomical Clock. I found a cafe away from the busiest part of the square, ate a quick lunch and collected my luggage.

The afternoon train from Prague to Vienna was comfortable and direct. Leaving after only one night felt rushed, but the carefully planned morning still gave me a memorable view of the city.

By the time I arrived at Vienna Hauptbahnhof, it was dark. My room at a&o Wien Hauptbahnhof was close to the station, making the arrival and next departure easy.
