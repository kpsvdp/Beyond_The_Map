Day 5

Arrival near Hauptbahnhof

A simple station-to-room transfer

The direct train from Prague reached Vienna Hauptbahnhof after dark. Staying at a&o Wien Hauptbahnhof kept the arrival simple: I could walk from the station, check in quickly and avoid crossing an unfamiliar city with luggage.

The location also mattered for the onward journey. With Bratislava next, sleeping close to the main station protected the early departure and gave me more time for Vienna itself.

Day 6

A full Vienna day

Palaces, trams and the historic centre

I began at Schonbrunn Palace. Rather than trying to visit every paid section, I chose the main experience and spent time walking through the grounds.

From Schonbrunn, I used Vienna's underground and tram network to reach the Belvedere area. The public transport was organised and made the large city manageable without expensive taxis.

In the afternoon, I continued to the historic centre and St Stephen's Cathedral. I walked through the surrounding streets and followed part of the Ringstrasse, passing some of Vienna's most impressive architecture.

Food was more expensive here than in Krakow or Bratislava, so I balanced one traditional meal with simpler bakery and supermarket food. Apple strudel and coffee became a small break that felt like one of the most Viennese moments of the journey.

That evening, I returned to the hostel near Hauptbahnhof and prepared for the short early journey to Bratislava.
