🇵🇱 Poland travel story

From Krakow's Old Streets to Zakopane

The journey began with two flights into Krakow, then changed completely as the road climbed towards the Tatra Mountains.

Day 1

Newcastle to Krakow

Flights, airport rail and the first Old Town walk

I left Newcastle in the morning and flew to Amsterdam for my connection to Krakow. Because both flights were on one ticket, the transfer at Amsterdam Airport was straightforward. After finding the next gate and waiting for the second flight, I arrived in Krakow in the late afternoon.

Instead of taking an expensive taxi, I used the airport train to reach Krakow Glowny. The journey into the city was easy, and the main station placed me close to the historic centre and my hostel.

After checking in and leaving my bag, I walked through Rynek Glowny. The square was full of old buildings, lights and people moving between cafes and restaurants. I circled the Cloth Hall and continued towards St Mary's Basilica.

I expected to feel tired after two flights, but arriving in a new city gave me fresh energy. Pierogi in a small local restaurant became the simple first meal that properly began the journey.

Day 2

Krakow to Zakopane

Wawel, Kazimierz and the evening coach south

The following morning, I returned to the Old Town and walked towards Wawel Hill. From the castle grounds I could see the Vistula River and part of the city below before continuing to Kazimierz, with its historic streets, small shops and cafes.

At lunchtime, I chose a local milk-bar-style restaurant. The food was filling, affordable and better suited to the trip than eating beside the busiest tourist square.

Later, I collected my luggage and walked to Krakow MDA Bus Station. As the evening coach carried me towards Zakopane, the roads grew quieter and the landscape gradually changed.

I reached Zakopane Bus Station after dark and made my way to Willa Pod Bandera, my room for two nights. Krupowki Street felt completely different from Krakow: wooden buildings, food stalls and cold mountain air made it clear that I had entered the Tatra region.

I tried oscypek, the smoked mountain cheese for which the area is known, before returning to the room.

Day 3

A full Zakopane day

Mountain views and a deliberately slower pace

Zakopane was the first place on the journey where I slowed down. I began in the town centre and then travelled towards Gubalowka, where the ride up opened wide views across the town and towards the mountains.

The weather was cold, but the view made the journey worthwhile. Back in the centre, potato pancakes and hot tea were exactly what I needed after spending time outside.

Morskie Oko was tempting, but mountain conditions and limited winter daylight made it a serious plan rather than a casual walk. I chose the safer local experience and spent more time enjoying the town and mountain atmosphere.

That evening, I returned to Krupowki Street for dinner. The main street was busy while the side streets around it were much quieter.

Back at Willa Pod Bandera, I packed my bag because the early coach to Krakow and onward train through Ostrava would make the following day one of the longest travel days of the journey.

Memory

What stayed with me

Poland in one chapter

Krakow brought historic streets, easy public transport and affordable food; Zakopane replaced the city noise with mountain air and a slower rhythm.

The landscape changed as much as the atmosphere, and Poland made the opening of the journey feel like two completely different trips.
