My Brussels Travel Story

A comfortable high-speed train brought me from Paris into a smaller, relaxed capital filled with historic squares, decorative architecture and famous local flavours.

Arriving by rail

I travelled from Paris to Brussels by high-speed train. The journey was comfortable and brought me directly into the centre of the Belgian capital.

Brussels felt smaller and more relaxed than Paris. Its historic squares, decorative architecture and food culture made it an enjoyable destination for a shorter stay.

01

Discovering Brussels

Historic streets, city symbols and a futuristic landmark

The centrepiece of Brussels is the magnificent Grand-Place. The square is surrounded by ornate guildhalls and historic buildings. It looked particularly beautiful when illuminated in the evening.

A short walk from Grand-Place brought me to the famous Manneken Pis. The statue is surprisingly small, but it remains an important symbol of the city.

I also travelled to the Atomium, one of Brussels' most unusual attractions. Its giant connected spheres represent an enlarged iron crystal. Visitors can explore its exhibitions and enjoy views of the surrounding area.

A sightseeing bus can be useful for first-time visitors. More tour and family-attraction information is available from Visit Brussels.

02

Food in Brussels

Local flavours and simple ways to save

Brussels is famous for Belgian waffles, fries, chocolates and bakery products. Exploring the chocolate shops was one of the most enjoyable parts of my visit.

🧇 Food tip

Food around Grand-Place can be expensive. I found better-value options by walking away from the central tourist streets and using local food stalls and small cafés.

03

Getting around Brussels

Walking, metro, buses and trams

The historic centre is compact and easy to explore on foot. The metro, buses and trams are useful for visiting places such as the Atomium and locations outside the centre.

Brussels also has excellent international railway connections, which made returning to Paris straightforward.

Home

My Brussels impression

A welcoming capital that was easy to explore

Brussels was welcoming, historic and easy to explore. Grand-Place was the highlight, but the city's relaxed atmosphere, architecture and food made the whole visit memorable.

A short stay was enough to feel the contrast between grand architecture and Brussels' easy-going pace.
