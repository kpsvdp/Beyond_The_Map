My Paris Travel Story

My European journey began from the UK to Paris—a city of grand monuments, riverside walks, cafés and small moments that stayed with me.

The journey begins

My European journey began with a trip from the UK to Paris. Travelling by Eurostar was simple and comfortable, taking me directly from London St Pancras to the centre of the French capital.

Paris felt elegant from the moment I arrived. Its wide boulevards, historic buildings, cafés and famous monuments made every walk feel special.

01

Discovering Paris

Landmarks, art, river views and hilltop streets

My first major stop was the Eiffel Tower. Seeing it in person was unforgettable. Visitors can book a ticket to go up the tower or enjoy free views from the Champ de Mars and Trocadéro.

I then explored the Louvre Museum, home to an extraordinary collection of paintings, sculptures and historical objects. Because the museum is enormous, booking a timed ticket and selecting a few galleries beforehand is helpful.

A Seine River cruise was one of my favourite Paris experiences. It offered relaxing views of the city's bridges, riverside buildings and famous landmarks.

Montmartre gave me a different experience of Paris. Its narrow streets, artists and cafés created a charming atmosphere. At the top of the hill, I visited the Sacré-Cœur Basilica and enjoyed a panoramic view across the city.

Other memorable places included Notre-Dame Cathedral and the free rooftop terrace at Galeries Lafayette.

02

Food in Paris

Bakeries, cafés and better-value local choices

Paris is an excellent destination for anyone who enjoys bakeries and café culture. I tried croissants, crêpes, baguette sandwiches, cheeses and French pastries.

🥐 Food tip

Restaurants beside major attractions can be expensive. Local bakeries, supermarkets and cafés away from tourist areas usually provide better-value meals.

03

Getting around Paris

Walking, Metro travel and returning after Brussels

Walking is the best way to appreciate the city, while the Metro is useful for longer journeys. Contactless tickets and travel passes can make local transport easier.

After visiting Brussels, my itinerary brought me back to Paris. This second stay gave me time to revisit favourite neighbourhoods, relax in a café and explore attractions I had missed earlier.

Home

My Paris impression

The feeling I carried into the next part of the journey

Paris was artistic, energetic and full of character. It was not only the famous landmarks that made the city memorable, but also the small experiences—walking beside the Seine, discovering local bakeries and watching everyday Parisian life.

Paris became more than a list of landmarks; it became the atmosphere between them.
