Six Days Across Türkiye

A continuous journey from London through Istanbul, Cappadocia, Antalya and Pamukkale - told through airports, rooms, buses, local streets, memorable food and the moments that stayed with me.

The journey begins

My journey to Türkiye began early in the morning at a London airport. I carried only cabin baggage and kept my passport, visa, hotel confirmations and flight details inside my small backpack. I was tired, but the excitement of visiting a new country kept me awake while I waited at the gate.

After nearly four hours in the air, the plane began descending towards Istanbul. From the window, I could see buildings stretching far into the distance, divided by wide waterways and connected by bridges. Istanbul looked enormous before I had even landed.

Day 1

My first day in Istanbul

London flight · Sultanahmet room · Historic centre · Bosphorus

After completing passport control, I followed the signs for public transport. The airport was far from the historic centre, so the journey took some time. I changed from the airport connection to city transport and finally reached Sultanahmet.

My room was in a small hotel close to the main attractions. It was a simple room, but the location was perfect. I left my bag, washed my face and went outside immediately because I had only one day to explore Istanbul.

Within a few minutes, I reached Sultanahmet Square. The Blue Mosque stood on one side, while Hagia Sophia faced it from the other. The sound of people speaking different languages filled the square. Some visitors were taking photographs, while others were sitting near the fountain and watching the city move around them.

I entered the Blue Mosque after checking the prayer schedule. The atmosphere inside was peaceful. The blue tiles, high ceiling and soft light made the building feel much larger than it appeared from outside.

I then crossed the square and visited Hagia Sophia. Looking at the enormous dome, Islamic calligraphy and ancient mosaics in the same building felt strange and powerful. It was not simply a tourist attraction. It was a place where different periods of history remained visible together.

Later, I walked towards Eminönü and joined a short Bosphorus cruise. The ferry moved between the European and Asian sides of Istanbul. From the water, I saw mosques, palaces, bridges and old waterfront houses. Seagulls followed the boat while passengers watched the city from the open deck.

🍲 Food memory

When the cruise ended, I bought a warm simit from a street stall and later ordered Turkish lentil soup with fresh bread. I finished with Turkish tea and a small piece of baklava. The meal was simple and comforting after a long flight and several hours of walking.

That evening, I returned to my room in Sultanahmet. I could still hear traffic and people outside, but after the flight and the long walk through the city, I fell asleep quickly.

Day 2

From Istanbul to Cappadocia

Domestic flight · Göreme shuttle · Cave hotel · ATV sunset

The next morning, I checked out early and travelled back to the airport. My next flight was a domestic journey from Istanbul to Cappadocia. Depending on the flight schedule, travellers can use Kayseri or Nevşehir Airport. I had already arranged a shuttle from the airport to my hotel in Göreme.

As the shuttle moved closer to Cappadocia, the scenery slowly changed. The crowded streets of Istanbul disappeared, and unusual rock formations began appearing beside the road. When I finally reached Göreme, the town looked as if it had been built inside the landscape rather than on top of it.

My room was inside a cave-style hotel near the centre of Göreme. It had stone walls, warm lighting and a small window overlooking part of the town. It felt completely different from my Istanbul room.

After leaving my luggage, I visited the Göreme Open Air Museum. The site was filled with churches, monasteries and rooms carved directly into the rocks. Inside some of the churches, old religious paintings were still visible on the walls and ceilings.

In the evening, I joined an ATV sunset tour. We travelled along dusty tracks through the valleys, following the guide from one viewpoint to another. By the end of the ride, my clothes and face were covered in dust, but watching the sunlight change the colour of the rocks made it worthwhile.

🏺
Favourite food of the trip

Chicken-and-vegetable testi kebabı

The chicken and vegetables were slowly cooked inside a sealed clay pot. When the food arrived, the waiter carefully broke the pot open at the table. The meal was warm, rich and filling, but the way it was served made it special. It felt connected to Cappadocia rather than like an ordinary restaurant meal.

I returned to my cave room and went to bed early. Before sleeping, I set several alarms because the next part of the journey would begin before sunrise.

Day 3

Above the valleys of Cappadocia

Sunrise balloon · Turkish breakfast · Northern Cappadocia tour

While Göreme was still dark, a minibus collected me from the hotel and took me to the balloon launch area. Large balloons slowly filled with air as the burners lit up the darkness. Every few seconds, the flames made a powerful sound.

When the basket finally lifted from the ground, the noise below disappeared. The balloon moved slowly above the valleys while the sun began rising. Fairy chimneys, cave houses and winding paths appeared beneath us. Dozens of other balloons floated at different heights across the sky.

After landing, the minibus returned me to Göreme. I ate a traditional Turkish breakfast at the hotel with bread, cheese, olives, tomatoes, cucumbers, eggs, fruit and tea. I then joined a tour of northern Cappadocia.

During the day, I visited Uçhisar, Love Valley, Paşabağ, Avanos and Devrent Valley. Each place looked different. Uçhisar offered a wide view across the region, while Love Valley was filled with tall natural rock pillars. Paşabağ had some of Cappadocia's most recognisable fairy chimneys.

In Avanos, I watched a pottery demonstration and walked near the river. At Devrent Valley, the guide pointed towards rocks that looked like animals and familiar objects. Some shapes were easy to recognise, while others required more imagination.

🥗 Food memory

During the tour, I chose a vegetarian lunch with rice, roasted vegetables, salad and bread. That evening, I ordered vegetarian mantı filled with spinach and cheese and served with yoghurt, garlic and spiced butter. It was a warm and comforting meal after spending most of the day outside.

I stayed in the same Göreme room for a second night. Not changing hotels made the trip easier because I could leave my luggage safely while exploring Cappadocia.

Day 4

The long road to Antalya

Göreme coach · Changing landscapes · Kaleiçi · Mediterranean sunset

The following morning, I checked out and travelled to Göreme bus station. My next journey was an intercity bus from Cappadocia to Antalya. It would take most of the day.

I placed my cabin bag inside the luggage compartment but kept my passport, wallet, phone and charger in my backpack. The coach stopped several times during the journey, allowing passengers to use the facilities, buy food and stretch their legs.

The landscape changed throughout the drive. Cappadocia's dry valleys slowly disappeared, and greener land and mountain views began appearing. Travelling by bus took longer than flying, but it allowed me to see parts of Türkiye that I would otherwise have missed.

I reached Antalya in the afternoon and used local transport from the bus station to Kaleiçi. My room was close to the Old Town, and I had booked it for two nights. This meant I could leave my luggage there during the Pamukkale day trip.

After checking in and resting briefly, I walked through Kaleiçi's narrow streets. Traditional houses stood beside small hotels, cafes and shops. I passed through Hadrian's Gate and continued towards the marina.

As the sun began setting, I reached Hıdırlık Tower and Karaalioğlu Park. From there, I could see the Mediterranean Sea stretching towards the horizon. After the dry landscape of Cappadocia, Antalya felt warm, green and relaxed.

🥙 Food memory

For dinner, I tried Antalya piyaz with grilled vegetable skewers. Piyaz is prepared with beans, tahini, garlic and lemon. I had expected a basic bean salad, but its creamy dressing gave it a strong and distinctive flavour. The colourful grilled vegetables made the meal complete.

I returned to my room early because the following morning would begin before sunrise again.

Day 5

White terraces and an ancient city

Antalya bus · Pamukkale · Hierapolis · Late return

I left Antalya between five and six in the morning and travelled by bus towards Denizli. From there, I continued to Pamukkale using a local connection.

The journey was long, but the first view of Pamukkale's white terraces immediately caught my attention. The mineral formations looked like snow from a distance, even though the weather was warm.

I removed my shoes where required and walked carefully across the travertines. Warm water flowed over the white surface and collected inside shallow pools. Some areas were crowded, but walking further away gave me more space to enjoy the landscape.

From the terraces, I continued into the ancient city of Hierapolis. The ruins covered a much larger area than I had expected. I walked past the remains of baths, temples and old stone streets before reaching the Roman Theatre.

The theatre was the most impressive part of Hierapolis. Rows of stone seats rose above the stage, and the upper sections offered a wide view across the archaeological site.

I also visited Cleopatra's Antique Pool. The thermal water surrounded fallen Roman columns. Even visitors who did not swim could sit nearby and rest after walking around Hierapolis.

🫓 Food memory

For lunch, I ate gözleme filled with cheese and spinach and drank a cold ayran. The thin flatbread was fresh, light and easy to eat during a busy sightseeing day.

In the late afternoon, I began the long return journey to Antalya. By the time I reached Kaleiçi, it was already late. I was exhausted, but returning to the same room made everything easier. I did not need to check into another hotel or carry my luggage throughout the day.

Day 6

A quiet final morning beside the sea

Konyaaltı Beach · Final meal · Antalya Airport · London

The final morning felt much calmer. My flight to London was later in the day, so I had time to see one more part of Antalya.

I travelled to Konyaaltı Beach and walked beside the Mediterranean. The water looked clear, and the mountains rose behind the coastline. After several days of airports, buses, tours and early alarms, sitting near the sea felt peaceful.

🍞 Final meal

Before leaving, I ate a cheese-and-spinach pide with Turkish tea. The warm bread, melted cheese and savoury spinach made it a satisfying final meal.

I returned to the hotel, collected my luggage and used local transport to reach Antalya Airport. I arrived early enough to complete security and passport control without rushing.

Home

What stayed with me

The memories that remained after the journey ended

As the plane left Antalya and began its journey towards London, I thought about how different each part of the trip had been. Istanbul had given me crowded streets, mosques and the Bosphorus. Cappadocia had brought balloons, valleys and cave rooms. Pamukkale had combined natural beauty with an ancient city, while Antalya had offered old streets and the Mediterranean coast.

Six days was not enough to see everything, and the journey was far from relaxing. There had been early flights, long bus rides and tiring days. But every place had given me a completely different memory.

When I thought about the trip later, I did not remember the waiting at airports or the hours spent on buses. I remembered the first view of Hagia Sophia, the silence above Cappadocia, the white terraces of Pamukkale, the sunset in Antalya and the moment a clay pot was broken open to reveal my favourite chicken-and-vegetable pottery kebab.
