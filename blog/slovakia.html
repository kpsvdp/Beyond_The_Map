Day 7

Vienna to Bratislava

A short transfer and a full walking route

I left Vienna early and travelled to Bratislava. The journey between the two capitals was short, which made the change of country feel almost too easy.

After arriving, I went to Patio Hostel, left my bag and started walking. Bratislava's centre was much smaller than Vienna's, and most of the main places could be reached on foot.

I began with the Blue Church before heading towards the Old Town. I walked through narrow streets, passed Michael's Gate and stopped in one of the squares for bryndzove halusky.

Later, I climbed towards Bratislava Castle. The grounds offered wide views across the Danube and towards the modern parts of the city. Because the centre was compact, the day never felt rushed.

In the evening, I returned to the Old Town and walked back to Patio Hostel. Bratislava had been a short stop, but it worked perfectly between Vienna and Budapest.

Day 8

Onward to Budapest

A final morning and the southbound train

I kept most of the morning free for another short Bratislava walk. The compact centre meant I could revisit the river and Old Town without worrying about a long cross-city transfer.

After collecting my bag, I travelled to the railway station and boarded the train south to Budapest. Within one travel day, the small Slovak capital gave way to a larger and busier city.
