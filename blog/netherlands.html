Arriving at Centraal

After returning to Paris, I continued my journey to Amsterdam by international train. Arriving at Amsterdam Centraal was convenient because the station is close to the canals and the historic city centre.

Amsterdam immediately felt different from Paris and Brussels. Its narrow houses, bicycles, canals and bridges gave the city a calm but lively personality.

01

Discovering Amsterdam

Canals, Dutch art and a peaceful city park

One of my first activities was an Amsterdam canal cruise. The boat travelled beneath historic bridges and past traditional canal houses. It was a relaxing way to understand the city's layout and history.

I also visited the Rijksmuseum. Its collection includes major works of Dutch art and history, including paintings by Rembrandt and Vermeer. Reserving a ticket in advance is recommended.

After exploring the museums, I spent time in Vondelpark. The park provided a peaceful break from the busy streets and was ideal for walking and relaxing.

More museums, neighbourhoods, markets and attractions are available through the official I Amsterdam visitor guide.

02

Food in Amsterdam

Dutch snacks and convenient local choices

Amsterdam has traditional Dutch snacks and a wide selection of international food. I tried Dutch pancakes, stroopwafels, fries, cheeses and fresh sandwiches.

🥞 Food tip

Markets, food halls and bakeries were convenient for affordable meals. Restaurants beside major attractions were generally more expensive, so checking menus and prices before sitting down was helpful.

03

Getting around Amsterdam

Walking, trams, metro and bicycle awareness

Central Amsterdam is easy to explore on foot. Trams and metro services are convenient for longer journeys, and contactless payment is widely available.

Cycling is an important part of Amsterdam life. Visitors should pay close attention when crossing streets and avoid walking or standing in marked cycle lanes.

At the end of my stay, I travelled from Amsterdam back to the UK.

Home

My Amsterdam impression

The relaxed final chapter of the journey

Amsterdam was colourful, creative and relaxing. The canal cruise, historic architecture and museums were highlights, but simply walking beside the canals was equally enjoyable.

Three connected cities offered three different personalities—Amsterdam ended the journey with calm water, creative energy and memorable streets.
