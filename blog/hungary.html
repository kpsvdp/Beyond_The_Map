Day 8

Bratislava to Budapest

Arrival at Nyugati and the first Danube walk

I had most of the morning free in Bratislava before collecting my bag and travelling to the railway station. My train continued south to Budapest.

I arrived at Budapest Nyugati in the evening and used public transport to reach Vitae Hostel. Budapest immediately felt larger and busier than Bratislava. Trams crossed the city, metro stations linked distant districts and the Danube divided Buda from Pest.

After checking in, I went out for a first evening walk. The illuminated Parliament building and its reflections on the river were more impressive than I had expected.

I ended the evening with langos from a casual food place before returning to the hostel.

Day 9

Exploring Buda and Pest

Castle views, riverfront trams and a thermal bath

I began on the Buda side, walking through the Castle District and continuing to Fisherman's Bastion. From there, I could see Parliament across the Danube and much of Pest.

I continued past Matthias Church and walked down towards Chain Bridge. Crossing on foot gave me another view of both sides of Budapest.

Later, I visited the riverfront near Parliament and used the tram to cover more of the city. Budapest's transport system made it possible to move between distant sights without wasting too much time.

In the afternoon, I visited a thermal bath. After several long travel days, a few hours in the warm pools were exactly what I needed. I carried my own swimwear and checked the locker rules before entering, saving time and avoiding extra rental costs.

For dinner, I found a small restaurant away from the busiest tourist streets. Budapest was one of the places where I could easily have stayed longer, but Ljubljana was next.

Days 12–13

The protective return night

Ljubljana back to Budapest and the flight home

I returned from Ljubljana by direct coach one day before the flight. It was not the most exciting part of the route, but it protected the journey from road delays and removed the stress of a fragile same-day connection.

After arriving, I checked into a hostel with a straightforward airport connection. I bought a simple dinner, reorganised my bag and saved offline copies of the flight details.

On the final morning, I used Budapest's airport transport and reached the terminal with enough time for security and boarding.

As the flight left Hungary, I thought about the Parliament reflections, the view from Fisherman's Bastion, the thermal pools and the decision to return early enough to keep the journey safe.

Memory

What stayed with me

Hungary in one chapter

Budapest combined the largest city experience of the route with one of its smartest practical decisions: returning for a final protected night before flying home.

The Danube views made Budapest memorable; the return-night buffer made the whole route dependable.
