Day 10

Budapest to Ljubljana

A direct coach and the first river walk

I took a direct coach from Budapest to Ljubljana. The journey was long, but avoiding unnecessary train changes made it simpler. I kept water and food in my small bag and used the travel time to rest.

When I arrived, the difference was immediate. Ljubljana felt smaller, quieter and greener than Budapest. After checking into my hostel, I walked towards Preseren Square and Triple Bridge.

The Ljubljanica River ran through the centre, with cafes and colourful buildings on both sides. I followed the water towards Dragon Bridge and returned through the Old Town.

For dinner, I tried struklji and finished with a slice of potica. Ljubljana did not have the grand scale of Vienna or Budapest, but its relaxed atmosphere made it one of the easiest cities to enjoy.

Day 11

Castle and Old Town

Market, funicular, park and an unhurried evening

I started at Central Market before walking through the historic centre. The city was compact enough that I rarely needed public transport.

Later, I travelled up to Ljubljana Castle. Walking was possible, but the funicular saved time. From the top, I could see the rooftops, surrounding hills and mountains in the distance.

After returning to the city, I crossed Cobblers' Bridge and walked towards Tivoli Park. The pace was much calmer than Prague or Vienna.

I had time to sit beside the river, drink coffee and watch the city without checking the clock every few minutes. In the evening, I walked through Metelkova before returning to the hostel.

Day 12

Back to Budapest

The buffer journey before the flight home

I left Ljubljana by coach and returned to Budapest. It was not the most exciting travel day, but it was an important decision.

Spending one final night in Budapest instead of trying to reach the airport from Ljubljana on the same day protected the home flight from road delays and removed the stress of a fragile connection.

Memory

What stayed with me

Slovenia in one chapter

Ljubljana gave the route a calmer final sightseeing chapter: a walkable centre, green spaces, castle views and enough time to sit beside the river.

The city was smaller than the capitals before it, but its relaxed pace made it one of the easiest places to remember.
