Day-by-day route

The complete travel plan

DAY 1

Newcastle → Paris

Fly NCL to CDG, transfer into Paris, check in, visit the Louvre, take a Seine cruise and finish with an evening Eiffel Tower view. Move the Louvre to Day 2 if the flight is delayed.

£179–£199
DAY 2

Paris landmarks

Morning walk or cycle, Eiffel Tower, Sacré-Cœur and Montmartre, Moulin Rouge exterior, Notre-Dame area and Pont Alexandre III.

£79–£98
DAY 3

Paris → Brussels → Paris

Early Eurostar to Brussels, historic centre, optional hop-on hop-off bus, Atomium area, Manneken Pis, Grand-Place, food stops and a confirmed evening train back to Paris.

£246–£297
DAY 4

Paris → Amsterdam

Final Trocadéro view, Galeries Lafayette rooftop, direct Eurostar to Amsterdam, hotel check-in and an evening canal walk.

£105–£235
DAY 5

Amsterdam sightseeing

Canal cruise, Jordaan and Nine Streets, then choose either the Rijksmuseum or Vondelpark before Dam Square and dinner.

£80–£110
DAY 6

Amsterdam → Newcastle

Begijnhof, flower market or a free central walk, then Amsterdam Centraal to Schiphol and the flight to Newcastle. Allow about three hours at Schiphol.

£86–£201

  • Amsterdam: 2 nights, plan £160–£280. Sloterdijk or Oost may be better value than the canal core.
  • Visa-ready proof: each confirmation should show full name, property address, dates, reference number and cancellation or payment status.
Food

Simple six-day meal plan

  • Paris: croissant, baguette or crêpe, omelette or quiche, ratatouille, falafel or a chicken meal.
  • Brussels: waffle, frites, Belgian chocolate and a vegetarian or chicken main.
  • Amsterdam: poffertjes, stroopwafel, vegetable toast and chicken satay; ask specifically for vegetarian bitterballen if wanted.
  • Budget: the six daily lines total £236–£291. Keep £311 as a cautious cap.
