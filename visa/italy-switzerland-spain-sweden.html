🇮🇹 Italy · correct application country

One Italy-led Schengen application

For an Indian passport holder legally resident in the UK, this four-country route needs one short-stay tourism application through Italy. Italy has the longest stay and is the first Schengen entry. Switzerland, Spain and Sweden must appear in the same form, itinerary, bookings, insurance and cover letter.

Country logic

Why Italy controls this route

The competent Schengen state is normally the main destination. Italy has the most nights and is also the first entry. A uniform visa issued for the disclosed itinerary can cover the other Schengen stops.

Application process

Six clear steps

  1. Check the official visa route. Use Visa for Italy to confirm a C-type tourism visa is required for your nationality and UK residence.
  2. Build future bookings. Hold the complete arrival, internal route, 13 nights and return to Newcastle with matching dates.
  3. Prepare the form. Follow the current Italy VFS UK instructions and print/sign exactly where required.
  4. Book VFS. Italy appointments in the UK are handled through VFS Global. Use only the official portal.
  5. Attend in person. Bring the ordered file, original passport, appointment proof, payment method and biometrics if required.
  6. Check the returned visa. Verify name, passport number, validity dates, entries and permitted days before travel.
Timing and fee

Plan for delay

  • Application window: normally no earlier than 6 months before the intended trip and no later than 15 days before it.
  • Normal processing: 15 calendar days under the common Schengen rules.
  • Possible extension: up to 45 days when more examination or documents are needed.
  • Adult fee: €90, plus the current VFS service charge. Premium lounge, courier and SMS are optional extras, not visa requirements.
  • Practical advice: apply early enough for transport time and unexpected requests; do not rely on the 15-day minimum.
Document order

Italy-led tourism checklist

1. Form and appointment

Completed and signed short-stay form, VFS appointment confirmation, checklist and fee/payment documents requested by the current portal.

2. Passport

Signed passport issued within the previous 10 years, valid at least 3 months after leaving Schengen, with at least 2 blank pages; add bio-page and previous-visa copies.

3. Photograph

Recent ICAO-compliant photo in the size and format stated by Italy VFS UK.

4. UK immigration status

UK eVisa/status proof and a fresh share code or status printout. Your status must allow you to return to the UK after the trip.

5. Complete transport

NCL–Milan; Italy–Switzerland–Italy rail; Milan–Pisa–Rome; Rome–Barcelona; Barcelona–Palma ferry; Palma–Stockholm; Stockholm–Krakow–Newcastle.

6. Every night

Milan 2 nights, Interlaken 2, Milan 1, Rome 2, Barcelona 1, ferry 1, Palma 1 and Stockholm 3. Names and dates must match the passport and route.

7. Insurance

Named policy valid throughout all Schengen countries for the full stay with at least €30,000 cover for emergency treatment, hospitalisation and repatriation. Confirm paragliding and scuba are covered.

8. Financial proof

Prepare the latest 3 complete monthly personal current/savings statements with transactions and balance, plus 3 payslips if employed, unless the live checklist requests a different period.

9. Work or study ties

Employer letter and approved leave, or current institution letter and approved absence/vacation. Add contract or enrolment evidence where useful.

10. Cover letter

One letter addressed to the Italian visa officer explaining the four-country route, costs, funding, activity insurance and return to the UK.

Financial proof

Reference amount versus real cost

  • Italian reference calculation: for one person staying 11–20 days, the published formula is €51.64 fixed + €36.67 per day.
  • Fourteen days: the formula gives €565.02. This is a minimum reference, not a guaranteed approval balance.
  • Real planning target: the site estimates £2,975–£3,725 including reserve. Your accessible funds must credibly cover unpaid costs and normal UK commitments.
  • Statements: use personal current or savings accounts with normal activity. Explain any large recent deposit with evidence.
  • Credit cards: do not rely on an unused credit limit as savings. A credit-card statement may support spending access only if the current checklist accepts it.
Employee or student

Use the route that is actually true

  • Employee: employer letter showing role, start date, salary, approved leave, expected return and signer contact; add 3 recent payslips and contract if useful.
  • Student: institution letter showing course, enrolment, course dates and that the travel falls in vacation or approved absence.
  • Self-funded: state this clearly and connect the trip cost to your own salary and savings.
  • Sponsored: add sponsor letter, ID, relationship evidence, recent bank statements and income proof; do not call it self-funded.
  • Return ties: explain exactly when you return to work or study in the UK.
