Country logic

Why France controls the file

The competent country is normally the main destination. France has the longest stay and is also the first Schengen entry. A uniform Schengen visa can cover the disclosed Belgium day trip and Netherlands stay.

Application process

Five steps

  1. Complete France-Visas. Create the short-stay tourism application, list France, Belgium and the Netherlands, and print the form, receipt and personalised checklist.
  2. Reserve the complete trip. Hold NCL–Paris, Paris–Brussels–Paris, Paris–Amsterdam and Amsterdam–Newcastle, plus all five hotel nights.
  3. Book TLScontact. Follow the official France-Visas UK route. Centres are in London, Manchester and Edinburgh.
  4. Attend in person. Bring the ordered file, passport, appointment confirmation, payment method and biometrics where required.
  5. Track and check the visa. Do not finalise non-refundable travel until the passport is returned and the visa details are correct.
Timing and fees

Plan early

  • Application window: up to 180 days before travel. EU guidance says at least 15 days before travel.
  • France UK advice: allow at least 20 working days. After transfer to the London Consulate, cases are generally processed in 2–15 working days, but delays vary.
  • Adult fee: €90. The France TLS basic fee is €40–€55 per appointment, collected in GBP.
  • EU processing rule: normal time is 15 days and may extend to 45 days for detailed examination.
Document order

Common France-led checklist

1. Application

Signed France-Visas form, receipt/checklist and TLS appointment confirmation.

2. Passport

Signed, issued within 10 years, at least 2 blank visa pages and valid at least 3 months after leaving Schengen.

3. Photograph

Recent ICAO-compliant photo in the size requested by the centre.

4. UK immigration status

UK eVisa/status printout and share-code letter; status must allow return after the trip. Add UK address proof if requested.

5. Route

NCL–Paris, Paris–Brussels–Paris, Paris–Amsterdam and Amsterdam–Newcastle reservations with matching dates.

6. Accommodation

Paris 3 nights and Amsterdam 2 nights. No missing night and no false Belgium hotel.

7. Insurance

Named certificate covering all Schengen countries for the full trip, at least €30,000, including emergency treatment, hospitalisation and repatriation including death.

8. Financial proof

Last 3 complete monthly personal current/savings statements, visible transactions and balance, plus 3 payslips if employed.

9. Work or study ties

Employer letter with approved leave, or current institution letter and approved absence/vacation confirmation.

10. Cover letter

One France-addressed letter explaining all three countries, funding, accommodation and the return to the UK.

Financial proof

Funds and bank statements

  • France reference: €65 per day with hotel booking. Six days = €390.
  • Without hotel proof: €120 per day. Six days = €720.
  • Reality: these are reference amounts, not a guaranteed bank-balance target. The account must also credibly cover unpaid hotels, transport, food and normal commitments.
  • Use: personal current or savings statements with transaction history. Explain any large recent deposit with evidence.
  • Do not rely on: a credit-card limit as savings. For this France-led file, use accessible cash funds and stable income evidence.
Employee or student

Choose the true route

  • Employee: official employer letter showing role, salary, start date or contract, approved leave, expected return and signer contact; add 3 recent payslips.
  • Student: official institution letter showing course, duration, enrolment and approved absence or vacation dates.
  • Sponsored student: sponsor letter, sponsor’s 3-month statements and 3 payslips, ID and relationship proof.
  • Both: show UK immigration status, address and a clear reason to return after the trip.
