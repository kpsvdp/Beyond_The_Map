Hungary VFS UK
Likely decision

Hungary appears on about five calendar days including the return from Slovenia; Poland appears on about four. Confirm the final itinerary before booking. If the longest stay changes, the application country changes.

Application steps

Six clear actions

  1. Finalise the real route. Cover every transport leg and all twelve room nights.
  2. Count days by country. Confirm Hungary remains the main destination.
  3. Use the Hungary VFS UK route. Select short-stay tourism and complete the current form.
  4. Order the evidence. Passport, UK status, itinerary, rooms, transport, insurance, finances and occupation.
  5. Attend in person. Submit documents and biometrics unless the mission confirms an exemption.
  6. Track and check. Avoid non-refundable extras until the passport returns and the visa details are correct.
Core rules

Passport, timing and insurance

  • Passport: issued within the previous 10 years, at least two blank pages, valid at least three months after Schengen departure.
  • Timing: applications can generally be lodged up to six months before travel and should be submitted at least 15 days before departure.
  • Processing: normally 15 calendar days, but it may extend to 45 days.
  • Insurance: all Schengen states and all travel days, with at least €30,000 for emergency care and repatriation including death.
  • Stay limit: this plan uses about 13 Schengen days; earlier and later visits also count toward the 90/180 rule.
Document order

Visa-ready checklist

1. Appointment

Correct category, centre, applicant name and confirmation.

2. Application form

Signed; all dates and entries match the evidence.

3. Passport and copies

Validity, issue date, blank pages and requested copies checked.

4. UK status

Printed eVisa/status evidence and a fresh share code if required.

5. Photo

Recent and compliant with the mission/VFS specification.

6. Cover letter

Explains Hungary as main destination and every country/leg.

7. Day itinerary

Cities, activities, transport, rooms and costs.

8. Travel bookings

UK entry/exit plus every intercity train and coach.

9. Accommodation

All twelve nights with name, address and dates.

10. Insurance

Schengen-wide, full dates and at least €30,000 cover.

11. Bank statements

Latest three months, current account, stable balance.

12. Work or study

Leave/study letter and three payslips when employed.

13. Previous visas

Copies of prior visas and travel history where requested; disclose refusals when the form asks.

14. Explanations

Sponsor pack, unusual-deposit source or relationship evidence only when relevant.

Financial proof

Use stable accessible funds

  • Show three complete months of personal UK current-account statements; add savings if they fund the trip.
  • Match payslips to salary credits and explain unusual deposits with source evidence.
  • Do not borrow money to manufacture a closing balance.
  • Do not rely on a credit-card limit as the primary evidence.
  • Sponsored applicants need a full sponsor pack and should still explain their own situation.
Employment or study

Show why you return

  • Employee: signed headed letter with role, start date, salary, approved leave and return to work; add three payslips.
  • Student: recent institution letter with enrolment, course, attendance/status and approved holiday or no term conflict.
  • Self-employed: accountant/solicitor letter, HMRC/company evidence and required business/personal statements.

Final warning

Consistency matters more than volume. Country counts, dates, room nights, tickets, insurance, leave dates and requested visa dates must all match. A thick file with contradictions is weaker than a shorter coherent file.

This is planning guidance, not legal advice or a visa guarantee. Recheck the live competent-mission checklist before submission.
