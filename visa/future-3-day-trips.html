These countries: visa dashboard

Schengen, conditional exemptions, separate visa systems and official application links for an Indian passport holder living in the UK.

Visa map for my passport

Schengen visa: Portugal, Iceland, Denmark, Norway, Finland, Romania, Greece, Croatia and Lithuania. One valid Schengen visa can cover these countries within its dates, entry count and permitted stay. I will apply to the country that is the genuine main destination, not the country with the easiest appointment.

Conditional visa-free entry: Albania and Moldova can accept qualifying valid UK residence/visa documents. Serbia accepts a valid UK visa but its published residence-permit exemption is framed differently, so I will verify my exact UK eVisa before relying on it.

Separate visa systems: Greenland requires specific Greenland validity; Ireland normally requires a Short Stay C visa for a long-term UK resident; Algeria requires a consular visa; Morocco and the Moroccan-administered part of Western Sahara use Morocco's eVisa route when the UK-residence conditions are met.

Core visa document pack

  • Current Indian passport plus copies; for Schengen, it will have been

    issued within 10 years, contain blank pages and remain valid at least three months beyond the intended Schengen departure.

  • Proof of lawful UK residence: UK eVisa printout/status evidence,

    visa-purpose share code where requested, and proof that I can return to the UK after the trip.

  • Signed application form, compliant photo, honest cover letter and a

    simple day-by-day itinerary.

  • Return flight/transport reservations and accommodation evidence

    covering every night. I will prefer refundable bookings until approval.

  • Recent bank statements, payslips and an employment letter/approved

    leave letter, or student enrolment and absence letter. I will explain large deposits instead of hiding them.

  • Travel medical insurance valid for the destination and activities.

    Schengen applications need at least €30,000 cover across the whole Schengen area.

  • Financial logic: there is no universal magic bank balance for every

    country. I will show ordinary income, the full trip cost and a sensible cushion. A credit-card limit alone is not strong proof of available travel funds.
